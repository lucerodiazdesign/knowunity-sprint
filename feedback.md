# Voice Recall — interview findings

Three directional moderated sessions to pressure-test the flow and controls before Final Submission. Not a formal validation study — the goal was sharp craft feedback and a read on how people orient to the step.

- **Participants:** 3 (1 on-audience, 2 off-audience)
- **Format:** Remote, moderated walkthrough
- **Confidence findings:** qualitative

> **Scope of claims.** These were informal interviews with a small group, read as directional signal, not validation. The 1–5 before/after confidence protocol wasn't run, so no deltas are reported. Because the sessions were guided walkthroughs, participants narrating their intent and waiting for a go-ahead is expected — it shouldn't be read as friction.

---

## What's working

**Text is a real input, not a fallback.** Two of three found it unprompted. The one genuinely reluctant speaker preferred it in real use — support for treating text as an equal path.

**Voice-vs-text preference is dispositional.** The split ran by temperament, not by situation. The most on-audience participant preferred speaking and named exactly why:

> "I genuinely like to talk… when I write I lose the thread a bit, so talking helps me a lot."

## Prioritized issues

| # | Issue | Signal | Size |
|---|-------|--------|------|
| 01 | **The pause reads as loading, not retrieval.** The beat before Knowie replies felt like the system working — "like ChatGPT with a longer question" — rather than the student's own moment to recall. This is the core mechanic, so it's the one to get right. | 2 of 3 explicit · 1 adjacent | **L** |
| 02 | **Hint vs. answer is indistinct.** Reni couldn't tell the hint apart from the answer. It's the mechanic that keeps a miss from collapsing straight into a reveal, so worth making the two visibly different. | 1 clean · 1 soft | **M** |
| 03 | **The "why / ¿Por qué?" entry is unclear.** Dean read the explanation drawer as a different-looking place than the main chat and wanted one consistent drawer; Tiara misread what it does, guessing it reopens the mic. Two different reads of one affordance. | 2 of 3 | **M** |
| 04 | **Control-label & IA cluster.** "Not relevant → Skip" (all three), "Replay → Play," "Erase → re-record," and grouping mic vs. text as competing inputs. The cheapest, highest-confidence wins in the set. | Skip 3 of 3 · others mixed | **M** |
| 05 | **Mic CTA legibility at test start.** The mic reads too dark to notice and the status icon reads as "next test." Proposed fix: "?" for the current step, "✓" for done. A clean, cheap entry-point tweak. | 1 of 3 | **M** |
| 06 | **Knowie's tone is inconsistent.** Collaborative in places, detached in others. Preference-level — one consistent character to aim for later. | 1 of 3 | **S** |

**Size key:** L — concept-level · M — usability friction · S — preference-level

*Preserve as-is: text-as-equal-input and the dispositional voice/text split both confirm the "equal modalities" principle.*
